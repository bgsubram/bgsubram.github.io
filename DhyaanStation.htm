02 user research and persona

  • I conducted interviews and surveys to identify the need for the app and the pain points, opportunity and insights

  • I interviewed 4 people of age groups between 30-55 years, to understand their need for the app

 

Qualitative research - Demographics

4  total participants

2 female, 2 male
Ages range between 30-55

All work in IT companies and want to spend quality time to unwind from their daily stress.

To align to the key functionalities with users needs, I set up my persona. While developing the persona, I kept in mind the major requirements and the key take away points of my User Research and it all summed up to the persona below

Whiteboarding the idea:

  • "I prefer to create playlists and listen to Affirmations or quietly close my eyes and Meditate during my commute on the Public Transit - I spend an hour in the subway where there is no network."

     

    Behavior:

    Time management and proper utilization of resources characterizes her user behavior. Whenever possible, she tries to be herself and grab a quiet moment or two as she never gets the so called "Me" time. She also loves Yoga and is lazy to practice all by herself, she wants an app where she could do Yoga while listening to the instructions.

     

    Pain Points:

    • Misleading Apps which cannot be used offline

    • Flawless navigation and playlist creation options

     

    Motivations:

    • Desire to use her commute time for her benefit 

    • Have a no screen time and meditate

    • Do Yoga on weekends without having to look atthe screen but listening to instructions

    36, Busy Mom and IT Manager

Project Learning SO FAR

  • Benefits of early user research

  • I cannot design a product users love without the help of the people who will actually use it!

  • This is important to understand the market and users, what do they need and how our product is going to help them!

  • Before any implementation, building lightweight wire-frames or clickable prototypes to walk your user through a given scenario really helps bring it to life.

  • Another important thing I have learned about this project was to communicate with developers as much as I can to create a better user experience
